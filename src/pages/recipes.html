Sicilian Cannoli

A lighter, baked version of the famous Sicilian cannoli, keeping the crispy texture without deep-frying. The ricotta filling is flavored with orange zest, vanilla, and chocolate chips.

Serving Size - 10
  • Preperation Time - 30 minutes (Plus 1 hour of chilling)
  • Cooking Time - 25 minutes

  • Ingredient List

    • 250g all-purpose flour
    • 30g sugar
    • 30g butter
    • 1 egg
    • 60ml orange juice
    • 1 pinch salt
    • 1 egg white
    • 500g ricotta cheese
    • 100g powdered sugar
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • 50g chocolate chips
    • 1 tbsp lemon zest

  • How to cook it!

    1. Mix flour, sugar, butter, egg, and milk (or orange juice) into a dough. Knead for 5 minutes, then chill for 1 hour.
    2. Roll & Cut: Roll out the dough thinly. Cut into circles, wrap around cannoli molds, and seal edges with egg white.
    3. Preheat the oven to 180°C/350°F.
    4. Bake for 15-20 minutes until golden brown. Let them cool before removing from molds.
    5. Mix ricotta, sugar, vanilla, orange zest, and chocolate chips.
    6. Fill a piping bag
    7. Pipe the ricotta mixture into the cooled shells. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.
Chicken Corbana Pasta

A creamy and delicious version of the Roman classic, replacing guanciale with chicken. The result? A rich and flavorful dish that stays true to the original.

Serving Size - 4
  • Preperation Time - 10 minutes
  • Cooking Time - 15 minutes

  • Ingredient List

    • 400g spaghetti
    • 150g seasoned chicken breast
    • 3 large eggs and 1 extra yolk
    • 100g Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
    • 1 pinch salt
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
    • Freshly ground black pepper

  • How to cook it!

    1. Boil Pasta: Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
    2. Cook Turkey Chicken: In a frying pan, heat olive oil and cook turkey bacon (or diced chicken) until crispy. Remove from heat.
    3. Make the Sauce: In a bowl, whisk eggs, extra yolk, grated Parmesan, and black pepper.
    4. Combine: Add the hot drained pasta into the pan with the turkey bacon. Quickly mix in the egg mixture, stirring fast to create a creamy sauce (use reserved pasta water if needed).
    5. Serve: Plate immediately with extra Parmesan and black pepper.
Osso Buco

A legendary dish from Lombard, slow-braised in a flavorful tomato and vegetable sauce. Served with a traditional saffron-infused risotto for a luxurious touch.

Serving Size - 4
  • Preperation Time - 20 minutes
  • Cooking Time - 2.5 hours

  • Ingredient List

    • 4 beef shanks (bone-in, about 2 inches thick)
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 2 carrots, diced
    • 2 celery stalks, diced
    • 5 garlic cloves, minced
    • 400g canned tomatoes, crushed
    • 500ml beef broth
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 tbsp flour
    • ½ tsp saffron (for risotto, optional)
    • Pinch of salt and pepper
    • Zest of 1 lemon
    • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

  • How to cook it!

    • Sear the Beef: Season beef shanks with salt, pepper, and flour. Heat olive oil in a pot and sear the meat until golden brown then remove.
    • In the same pot, cook onions, carrots, celery, and garlic until soft.
    • Add canned tomatoes and beef broth.
    • Return beef to the pot, cover, and simmer on low heat for 2 hours (or bake at 170°C).
    • Mix lemon zest, garlic, and parsley. Sprinkle over the dish before serving.
    • Serve: Traditionally paired with saffron-infused risotto or crusty bread.
